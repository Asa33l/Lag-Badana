Saving buffalo through drought

During the most recent famine last February, our team successfully rescued ten buffalo. Members of the conservancy were alerted to a herd between Raas-Kambooni and Kolbiyow that had been searching for water for over a week and was trying to reach a local well.

Our response was rapid: within a single day, the herd of ten buffalo was guided to safety and water. Further updates from this rescue can be found across our social media channels.

Protecting elephants in Lower Jubba

In Badhadhe District, Lower Jubba, we ran a series of educational workshops for local youth on the importance of protecting elephants — animals that often travel long distances in search of water between February and March.

Communities embraced the training. In past years, residents would try to drive elephants away from their wells; this year their migration happily coincided with the rains. Without the workshops, displaced elephants could have perished from thirst on the plains — an alarming possibility that was avoided.

Elephants of Lag Badana

There is no recent official census of elephants in Lag Badana, but our teams have recorded approximately 50 elephants in the area.

Main challenges facing elephants in Lag Badana–Bushbushle

🛡

Poaching

Illegal hunting for ivory and bushmeat threatens elephant survival, while reduced law enforcement makes protection difficult.

🪵

Habitat loss

Deforestation for charcoal and timber destroys vital elephant habitat.

🏭

Lack of park management

After the collapse of Somalia’s central government in 1991, park infrastructure and ranger services declined sharply.

⚡

Human–wildlife conflict

As habitat shrinks, elephants increasingly compete with communities over land and water.

🌧

Climate change

Shifting rainfall, drought and degraded wetlands reduce food and water for wildlife.

A biodiversity hotspot under pressure

Wildlife and the habitats they depend on in Lag Badana–Bushbushle National Park face severe threats from illegal hunting, deforestation, habitat degradation, climate change and rising human–wildlife conflict. Together these pressures erode the park’s rich biodiversity and the ecological balance of its land and coast.

Through active conservation, the Lag Badana Community Conservancy Trust (LCCT) is working to protect and restore critical wildlife habitats, strengthen biodiversity, and safeguard the ecological corridors that connect them.

The park remains a unique biodiversity hotspot — home to threatened species, tropical forests, coastal habitats and marine ecosystems.
